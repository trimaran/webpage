Sadun Anik

Sadun Anik is a research engineer in the Compiler and Architecture Research group at HP Laboratories. He joined HP Labs in 1993 after receiving his M.S. and Ph.D. from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.

At HP Labs, he has performed research on compiling for EPIC processors and on the organization of compilers for such processors. Sadun's current research interests include compiling for embedded processors and domain specific processor architectures, including systolic processors.

Sadun is responsible for some of the analysis and optimization modules of Elcor that are part of Trimaran. He also worked on the design and implementation of the Elcor intermediate representation and low level support modules that together provide the framework for Elcor.

Sadun no longer works with HP.
